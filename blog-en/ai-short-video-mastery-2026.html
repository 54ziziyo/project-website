The Key to Going Viral: AI Script Prompt Templates

Professional short-video scripts no longer rely on inspiration — they rely on logic. These two prompts help you produce content fast:

1. Golden 3-Second Hook Generator (Attention Grabber)
Prompt: I'm promoting "[product name, e.g., a motorcycle protective jacket]" to "[audience, e.g., beginner riders]." Give me 5 visually striking opening-action descriptions and 5 curiosity-sparking first lines (hooks).

2. 15-Second Viral Script Framework
Prompt: Write a 15-second Reels script on "[content focus]." Structure: first 3s hook -> 5s pain-point demo -> 4s solution -> 3s strong CTA (prompt viewers to comment to receive materials). Keep the tone lively with strong pacing.

The Three Golden Rules of 2026 Short-Video Production

1. The golden 3 seconds and the visual hook

Viewers decide whether to stay within the first 3 seconds. Beyond audio, a visual "surprise" is crucial. Use fast transitions, neon captions or a strong before/after contrast to make the thumb stop.

2. Optimize for muted viewing: active captions

Data shows over 80% of users watch short video on mute. What's popular in 2026 isn't stiff subtitles, but "dynamic captions" that bounce with the audio and use emphasis colors — effectively guiding the visual focus.

3. An interactive ending: make the algorithm love you

Don't just say "thanks for watching." Your CTA should trigger the algorithm — for example, "Comment '+1' to get the full checklist" or "Do you prefer A or B? Tell me in the comments." Comment count is a key signal for getting your video pushed into the traffic pool.
