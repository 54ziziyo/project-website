Why Your AI Output Doesn't Sound Like You

AI language models are trained on an average of human language. They know what a "good Instagram caption" looks like in general, but they don't know your niche, your audience, your tone, or your goals. If you don't tell them, they fill the gap with the safest average — and that's what generic output is.

The 4-Step Spoken Self-Intro: Give AI the Context It Needs

Once you have Typeless, do this before you paste any prompt into AI:

Open Typeless, speak into your mic, and cover these four things:

STEP 1
Who you are
Your identity, background, role. Example: "I'm a freelance social media manager, mainly working with small F&B brands in Taiwan."
STEP 2
What you're working on
Your current project or task. Example: "I'm planning 12 IG posts this month for a Japanese dessert shop."
STEP 3
What you want to achieve
The goal for this session. Example: "I want to increase engagement — get more people commenting about their flavor preferences."
STEP 4
What the output should look like
Format, tone, style. Example: "Keep it casual and comforting, not too salesy — end with a question that invites replies."

Typeless transcribes your spoken intro into clean text. Then paste that text at the top of your prompt before the actual instructions.

AI now knows who it's talking to — outputs become more relevant, more on-brand, and require far fewer back-and-forth revisions.
