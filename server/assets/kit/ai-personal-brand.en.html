IG Account Setup Tools

Bio writing, brand positioning, and Highlights strategy — turn your IG account into a salesperson that introduces you 24/7.

verified Official data｜The Instagram Bio limit is 150 characters. Including keywords in your handle, name field, and Bio significantly boosts your odds of appearing in search. Accounts that update their Bio link weekly see a click-through rate 50% higher than static links. (Source: Instagram Creator Academy / Sprout Social 2026)

Bio Structure Formula (the official recommended 4-line structure)

Line Purpose Example
Line 1 Who you are + whose problem you solve Helping small brands do social marketing with AI
Line 2 Social proof or concrete results Helped 30+ brands build their IG from scratch
Line 3 Personal flair or differentiation Weekly AI marketing tips
Line 4 (CTA) One single action + link 👇 Grab the free brand-building playbook
lightbulb Keywords work best in the "name field," because the name field is Instagram's primary search index field.

Blog Prompt Pack (20 prompts)

SEO title generation, article outlines, openers, and voice tuning — get AI to write in your style.

verified Google Search Central 2026｜Google AI Overview appears in 47–64% of search results. Articles that get cited by AI have clear claims, state the conclusion first, and show E-E-A-T signals. Articles that "bury the answer in long paragraphs" are far less likely to be cited than those that "state the conclusion first, then the reasons."

mail Part 4

Newsletter Templates (5 formats)

Each format is a ChatGPT prompt — fill in your content to generate the whole email (subject-line suggestions included).

verified Mailchimp / Brevo 2026｜Average open rate across industries is 21–29%. Using AI to optimize subject lines raises open rates by 26%. Best send times: Tuesday and Wednesday 8–10 AM. Putting just 1 CTA per email yields a 42% higher click-through than multiple CTAs.

Template 1 — Weekly Digest Format

ChatGPT Prompt
Write me a "weekly digest" newsletter (sharing what I learned this week). Brand name: [fill in] This issue's theme: [fill in] The most important thing this week: [fill in — your biggest learning / discovery / takeaway this week] Write it in a conversational, story-driven, genuine and unhyped voice, in the following structure: 1. Open by greeting readers + a one-line pointer to this issue's theme 2. "The most important thing this week": share in 150–200 words with a sense of story (what happened, what you thought, what you learned) 3. "3 things you might have missed": list 3 resources / tools / articles, each with a one-line reason to recommend + a link placeholder 4. "This week's new article": title + a summary under 30 words + a read-link placeholder 5. Sign-off (your name｜IG handle) At the end, also give me 2 subject lines (≤50 characters, no more than 2 punctuation marks, no "free" or "limited time").

Template 2 — Tips List Format

ChatGPT Prompt
Write me a "tips list" newsletter (5 tools / tips / resources). This issue's theme: [fill in, e.g. 5 AI tools that save you 2 hours] Target readers: [fill in] Output in the following structure: 1. A short opening, one line that gets straight to the topic 2. List 5 items, each: name + a one-line use + a concrete "why it's worth it" reason (don't just say "it's great") + a link placeholder 3. "This issue only": one extra little bonus + a link placeholder 4. Sign-off At the end, also give me 2 practical subject lines (≤50 characters, no over-the-top marketing words).

Template 3 — Personal Story Format

ChatGPT Prompt
Write me a "personal story" newsletter to help subscribers get to know me better. What I want to share: [fill in — a small, real event or turning point] Use a genuine, lightly polished, vivid voice, in the following structure: 1. Opening: enter through a concrete scene or detail so readers picture it immediately 2. Middle: what happened, my struggle and turning point, what I thought at the time 3. Ending: just one small, concrete takeaway 4. Invite readers to reply and engage + sign-off + a light P.S. (or a teaser for next issue) At the end, also give me 2 emotional, curiosity-sparking subject lines (≤50 characters).

Template 4 — Promo Announcement Format

ChatGPT Prompt
Write me a "promo announcement" newsletter (for when you have a product to sell). Product name: [fill in] Target audience: [fill in] What this product helps them achieve: [fill in 2–3 concrete results] Offer / limit: [fill in, e.g. until X date / limited to X spots] Use a direct, unhyped voice, with just 1 CTA in the whole email, in the following structure: 1. Open by saying directly that you have something to tell them 2. The product is open + who it's designed for 3. List the concrete results it achieves (use ✅) 4. One clear CTA (button text + link placeholder) 5. Give the uninterested an easy out + sign-off + a P.S. noting the urgency of spots / time At the end, also give me 2 subject lines (with moderate urgency, ≤50 characters, no "free" or "limited time").

Template 5 — Special Thanks Format

ChatGPT Prompt
Write me a "special thanks" newsletter — no selling, no tips, just a genuine thank-you. Why I want to thank these readers: [fill in — a real reason] Use a sincere voice that doesn't perform emotion, in the following structure: 1. Open by explaining this is a letter just to say thank you 2. A heartfelt paragraph: why I'm grateful for these readers 3. Let them know "having you here is not something I take for granted" 4. Invite them to reply and tell me "why they subscribed in the first place" + sign-off At the end, also give me 2 warm, personalized subject lines (≤50 characters).
